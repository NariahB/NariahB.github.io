Scholarship Opportunities

SMART Scholarship
The Science, Mathematics and Research for Transformation (SMART) Scholarship is directed under the DoD that gives college undergrad or graduate students pursuing in a STEM related field the opportunity to recieve full scholarship and gain full employemnt after completion of degree. For more information, click the link above.
NDSEG
The National Defense Science and Engineering Graduate (NDSEG) Fellowship is highly competitive and is given to those who are pursuing a doctorate degree in a STEM related field. The Fellowships lasts for four years (based on funding) and covers full tuition and mandatory fees. Those participating in the NDSEG Fellowship also recieve a monthly stipened and up to $1500 a year in medical insurance. For more information, click the link above.
The Women Techmakers Scholars Program
The Google Anita Borg Memorial Scholarship formaly known as The Google Anita Borg Memorial Scholarship was started to encourage women to excel in computing and technology and become active leaders and role models in the field. For more information, click the link above.
The Palantir Women in Technology Scholarship
The Palantir Women in Technology Scholarship was started in 2010 to support women pursuing careers in technology. Ten scholarship recipients recieve grants up to $10,000 to support their education, and selected participants are offered a Palantir internship.
The Generation Google Scholarship
The Generation Google Scholarship was established to support underrepresented groups purspue a career in computer science and become leaders in their field. Selected students will recieve $10,000 towards their education. High school and college students can apply. For more information, click the link above.





Internship Opportunities

URAP
The Undergrad Research Apprenticship Program (URAP) provides students with the opportunity to gain research experience with universities sponsored by the Army Research Office. Through this program, students will develop skills in Army critical science and engineering research in a university lab setting to prepare them for the next level in their academic and professional career. For more information, click the link above.
CQL
The College Qualified Leaders (CQL) program matches practicing DoD scientists with talented undergraduate students creating a direct mentor-student relationship, providing participants with training that is unparalleled at most colleges. CQL students receive firsthand research experience and exposure to DoD laboratories. CQL fosters desire in its participants to pursue further training and careers in STEM. Selected participants receive a stipend based on education and experience and may participate year-round. For more information, click the link above.
Advanced Courses in Cyber Security Bootcamp
The Advanced Courses in Cyber Security Bootcamp (ACE) seeks to produce the next-generation cyber security leaders from the top students at US colleges and universities, targeting the best in computer science, computer engineering, electrical engineering, mathematics, and engineering. ACE is a 10 week program that provides hands-on learning, which will help students produce problem solving skills needed in their professional and educational careers. For more information, click the link above.
